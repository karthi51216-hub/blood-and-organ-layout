What's In Your Blood?

Blood is made up of four components: Red Blood Cells, White Blood Cells, Platelets And The Protein-Rich Liquid That Suspends Them All, Plasma.

It’s Rare For A Patient To Require All The Blood Components At One Time, So A Blood Donation Is Usually Separated Into Three Parts For Transfusion - Red Blood Cells, Platelets And Plasma. This Means Patients Can Be Treated With Only What They Need. And Your Single Blood Donation Can Help More Than One Patient.

Because There’s No Substitute For These Lifesaving Components, Patients Around The Country Rely On Blood Donors To Help Save Their Lives.
